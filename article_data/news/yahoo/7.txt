WASHINGTON -- Hawaii's Kilauea is not your typical blow-the-top-off kind of volcano. It's been simmering and bubbling for about 35 years, sending superhot hot lava spewing up through cracks in the ground. This month's eruptions are more of the same, except the lava is destroying houses miles from the summit. The lava has already devoured more than two dozen homes in and around the neighborhood of Leilani Estates, reports CBS News' Carter Evans. By helicopter, he pointed out that you can see the destruction, but there aren't any firefighters nearby because there's nothing they can do.
