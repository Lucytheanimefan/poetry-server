Hero Southwest pilot says her husband was flight’s originally scheduled captain More

Captain Tammie Jo Shults, one of the pilots responsible for the safe landing of a Southwest flight last month after an engine exploded mid-air, leaving one passenger dead, was reportedly not initially scheduled to pilot the flight. Shults’ husband Dean, a fellow pilot for the airline, was originally scheduled as the captain on April 17, however Dean offered to do another flight instead so she’d be able to go to her son’s track meet, Shults said during an upcoming interview with ABC News. “Dean, being the amazing husband he is, said, ‘You go to the track meet, I’ll switch and take your trip.’ And so that’s why I was on the trip,” Shults said in the interview. “I traded for the trip with my husband.
