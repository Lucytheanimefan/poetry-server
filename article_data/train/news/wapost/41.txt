Tarana Burke and Amanda Seales on the ‘burden of black womanhood’
In Los Angeles last weekend, 6,000 activists, entrepreneurs, artists and celebrities gathered for the second United State of Women Summit, a gathering that aims to facilitate collaboration between groups working to improve the lives of women.

Two of those attendees were #MeToo founder Tarana Burke and “Insecure” actress and comedian Amanda Seales. The women had talked in passing, but never in depth — until The Lily brought them together at the summit to talk white privilege and black womanhood.

Here’s what they had to say.
